    What Are Viruses: Viruses are infectious agents that are too small to seen with light microscope and that are not cells. They have no cell nucleus, organelles or cytoplasm. When they invade susceptable cell (it has a functional receptor for a given virus.) The cell may or may not be able to support viral replication.

    Components of Viruses

    1. Nucleic Acid Core
    2. Capsid(known as protein coat)
    3. Envelope

    Nucleic Acids Peter Medawar, once described viruses as "a piece of bad news wrapped up in protein". Who is the bad news? . Let's get this right!

    What is protein? It is a polymer of amino acids joined by peptide bonds.

    What is Nucleic Acid: long polymer of nucleotide that encode genetic information and direct protein synthesis.

    Now back to our discussion, this is a bad news because Viruses uses there genomes to replicate themselves in the host cells. they either uses DNA or RNA, but never both.

    For the formation of viral protein, the replication depends on the expression of the viral genomes.
