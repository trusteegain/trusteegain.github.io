THE IMMUNE SYSTEM

The immune system is divided into two:

  1. The Innate nonspecific
  2. The adaptive specific immune system

  1. THE INNATE NONSPECIFIC SYSTEM:
This can be classified based on their first and second line of defense.
First line of defense: ---
  • physical defenses

    • which includes the skin and mucous membrane. their function is to deny pathogen from entrying.

  • Chemical defenses---

    • The complement system is the major site for chemical defense COMPLEMENT SYSTEM: They are group of plasma protein mediators that connects the innate and the adaptive system. They are not cellular defenders like neutrophils, eosinophils, basophils or mast cells. but they only assist them in destruction and elimination of pathogen. NOTE: MAC (membrane attack complex) causes pores on the membrane of the cell. This protein have two fragment the A and B fragment. There are 10-20 complement proteins (C1 - C9). In MAC when the C5-C9 combines together, where they forms small pores. Then when C8 and C9 they form larger pores. when the two complement proteins are cleaved by enzyme, only the B fragment is activated, while the A fragment is not activated. So the B fragment facilitates the reaction in the system.
